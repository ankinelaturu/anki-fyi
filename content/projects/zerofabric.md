# zerofabric.md

## What It Is

ZeroFabric is a runtime semantic capability coordination concept. The core idea is that systems can expose capabilities, constraints, signals, and abilities, while a fabric coordinates execution paths based on intent and context.

## Why I Explored It

Most workflow systems are designed as static flows. ZeroFabric explores what happens when the runtime can choose among governed capability paths while still respecting constraints, approvals, and observability.

## Technical Concepts

- Runtime capability matching
- Orbs exposing signals and abilities
- Governed execution paths
- Policies and approval constraints
- Semantic matching with deterministic execution
- Agentic workflow coordination
- Debuggable active intent paths

## Why It Matters

The future of agentic systems will not be free-form LLM execution. It will require governed capability spaces, inspectable paths, clear constraints, and deterministic execution boundaries. ZeroFabric is an exploration of that layer.

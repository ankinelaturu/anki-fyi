# OzMap

> AI-powered learning trails that turn topics into structured exploration maps.

## What It Is

OzMap is an AI-powered learning exploration platform that treats knowledge as a connected landscape rather than a collection of isolated pages.

A user starts with a topic and the system expands that topic into related concepts, dependencies, branches, prerequisites, and potential learning paths. Instead of repeatedly asking "what should I learn next?", the learner can visually navigate the subject and understand how ideas connect.

The project explores graph-oriented learning experiences where knowledge becomes something users can explore rather than simply search.

## Why I Built It

I have spent years thinking about learning systems, microlearning, educational tools, and knowledge organization.

Many AI systems are extremely good at generating explanations but provide very little guidance about where a learner should go next. The result is often a collection of disconnected answers.

OzMap explores a different model. Instead of generating a single response, the system attempts to generate a navigable structure.

The goal is not simply to answer questions. The goal is to help users build mental models.

## Technical Highlights

- Graph-based topic organization.

- Learning trail generation.
- Branching exploration paths.
- Dependency and prerequisite modeling.
- Interactive navigation and discovery.
- Structured progression through related concepts.

## Key Features

- Generate learning maps from a starting topic.
- Visualize concept relationships and dependencies.
- Discover prerequisites and related concepts.
- Create branching learning trails.
- Support progressive exploration and knowledge discovery.
- Foundation for quizzes, summaries, and review workflows.

## Technical Challenges

Knowledge graphs generated by AI can grow extremely quickly. The challenge is maintaining meaningful structure while avoiding information overload.

Another challenge is granularity. If concepts are too broad, exploration feels shallow. If concepts are too detailed, navigation becomes overwhelming.

The system must continuously balance discovery, structure, and usability.

## Why It Matters

OzMap represents my broader interest in AI-native education systems and knowledge navigation.

The project explores how AI can become a guide through information rather than simply a generator of information.

That idea continues to influence many of my other projects involving learning systems, knowledge mapping, retrieval, and interactive exploration.
